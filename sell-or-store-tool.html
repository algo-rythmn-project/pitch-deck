How it reads

Knob ① — Carry (F − S)
The shape of the curve, read straight off it and certain today. It sets sell against hedge. Above the flip point (carry = holding cost) storing to a locked forward beats selling; below it, sell.
Knob ② — View (E[S_T] − F)
A bullish opinion about the future, supplied by whoever holds one. It sets the naked fraction. Under the neutral (martingale) benchmark it is zero, and the model stores nothing naked.
The curve is an input, not something we predict. It can be today's market curve or anyone's forecast of it, for example a WIDEN / NARROW signal. The market has already priced in public fundamentals, so we sit downstream as the decision layer and translate a curve into an allocation, rather than compete on forecasting it. One identity keeps the two knobs honest: carry + view = E[S_T] − S. They split the total expected price move into a lockable part (carry) and a bet (view), so contango plus a bullish view is no contradiction; it means expecting the spot to rise by more than the curve already pays.
