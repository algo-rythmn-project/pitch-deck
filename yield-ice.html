The structural problem

Government crop data is retrospective. HarvestAlpha is predictive.

USDA's WASDE, NASS Crop Production, and Statistics Canada Principal Field Crops reports are the consensus inputs the entire ag-commodity complex prices off.

But the data they publish reflects field conditions and farmer surveys gathered weeks before release. By the time a number prints, the crop has already evolved past it.

That lag is the window. It is also the specific window in which option implied volatility rises, hedging demand peaks, and directional conviction is incredibly scarce.

The contract & the franchise

Canola is ICE's contract. The signal sharpens it.

  • ICE Canola (RS) is the global price discovery benchmark for the world's most concentrated oilseed origin.
  • Canada supplies roughly 60% of internationally traded canola — a single-origin contract with maximum yield sensitivity.
  • Standard elasticity: a 1% Canadian production surprise can move RS futures 3–5%. The 2021 episode was the live demonstration.
  • An ICE-branded yield signal — or a co-developed yield index — deepens engagement with the exact desks that already trade the contract.

Where the signal is most valuable

Vol rises into every report. Then it collapses.

Implied volatility in ag-commodity options rises in the roughly ten days before each WASDE and StatCan release as desks hedge binary uncertainty. After the print, vol collapses — the well-documented "vol crush."

The HarvestAlpha signal lives inside that window. When the market is actively searching for directional conviction and option premium is rich, our customers already hold the input.
