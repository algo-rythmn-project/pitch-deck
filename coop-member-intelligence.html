02 Executive Summary
The product, in one frame

Two audiences. One product.

Summary

Co‑op Member Intelligence is an agentic AI platform we sell to a co-op's enterprise — credit committee, procurement, and trading desks — and that forecasts at the level of each individual farmer-member. The co-op pays for the platform; the platform thinks one member at a time.

The Enterprise Buyer

The co-op itself — a billion-dollar institution that finances inputs, supplies seed and fertilizer, and trades aggregated grain on behalf of thousands of members. Buys software through an annual innovation budget, sponsored by a Diretor de Inovação or Diretor Comercial.

The Operational Unit

The individual farmer-member — a farm with its own credit history, planted area, and likely input order. The co-op's decisions only get smart if they're grounded in member-by-member signal, not aggregate guesses.

05 What We Build
The product

Three models that feed up
from the member to the enterprise.

Each layer takes member-level signal as input and delivers an enterprise-level decision as output. That's the whole architecture.

01

Member-level default-risk → Credit Committee decisions

Per-member, year-ahead probability of repayment difficulty, built from credit history, plot-level satellite data, weather risk, and commodity exposure. The credit committee uses it to size and price each member's input financing.

02

Member-level input demand → Procurement & Trading positions

Member-by-member predictions of fertilizer, seed, and defensives demand — aggregated up so procurement negotiates better contracts, and the trading desk sizes its grain position knowing what 5,000 farms will actually deliver.

03

Conversational layer → Relationship Managers & (optional) Members

An agentic chat interface in Portuguese. Internal users — relationship managers, credit analysts — ask plain-language questions across the member base. Optionally, the co-op resells a member-facing version as a premium service.

11 Engagement & Risk
How we de-risk this for the co-op

The objections we hear — answered up front.

Co-ops buying mission-critical AI from a foreign vendor have the same questions every time. Here's how the engagement is structured so none of them are deal-breakers.

◆

Data sovereignty

Models train and run inside the co-op's own cloud environment (AWS São Paulo region or Azure Brazil South). Member data never crosses jurisdictions. Co-op retains full control and can revoke access at any time.

◆

IP & model ownership

Models trained on the co-op's data become the co-op's property at production cutover. We license the underlying agentic framework; the co-op owns the fine-tuned weights and all derived outputs.

◆

Exit path & vendor risk

All models, prompts, and data pipelines deliverable in escrow at contract signing. If we disappear, the co-op's team or any successor vendor can run the system. No black boxes.

◆

Local presence

Pilot runs remote-first. On year-one platform conversion, we commit to a Brazilian operating partner or local hire for embedded support — named in the contract.

◆

Accuracy guarantee

Pilot fee is tied to a measurable accuracy threshold against the co-op's current model. If we don't beat the baseline by 15% on held-out data, the pilot fee converts to credit toward future work.

◆

Governance fit

We brief the co-op's Conselho de Administração on request. Member-facing rollouts are opt-in. No data crosses to other co-ops or third parties — ever, contractually.
