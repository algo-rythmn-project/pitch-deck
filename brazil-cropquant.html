How It Works

Not a crop model.
A curve-shape signal that reads the storage reward.

The futures curve encodes market consensus on supply timing and carry — relevant to anyone pricing, hedging, or lending against grain. CropQuant reads that shape, quantifies it as a factor, and issues WIDEN or NARROW only when the signal clears a random-walk benchmark.

1
Step 1 · The Shape

Curve steepness encodes the market’s carry signal

An upward-sloping curve signals a storage reward — deferred contracts above nearby. A flat or inverted curve signals the opposite. That deferred-minus-nearby spread is the raw input.

2
Step 2 · The Factor

A curve-shape factor model, re-estimated as history accumulates

The spread becomes a standardised curve-shape factor. A rolling model, re-estimated on all available history, predicts whether that factor grows or shrinks next month — benchmarked at every step against a random walk.

3
Step 3 · The Signal

Fires only on the handful of high-confidence days each year

Days are ranked by predicted move size. Only high-confidence days fire — roughly ~74 across 14 years of out-of-sample soybean history, about 5–6 per year. Low frequency, high signal-to-noise.
