Where the data
actually comes from.

A one-page answer to the question Telus will ask first. Read the black box aloud if you freeze.

The answer
Three sources — your proprietary data, external authoritative data, and synthetic data for simulations only.
Real recommendations are grounded in the first two. Synthetic data is used only for "what-if" scenarios and pre-connection demos — never for live advice. Every output carries a trace showing which source each data point came from.

01The three sources

If they want detail, walk them through these.
Source 1 · Client
Proprietary data
Your own databases, ERP, IoT telemetry, and historical records. The ground truth about your operations.
Used forThe "what is" in every recommendation. Historical outcomes. Current state.
Source 2 · External
Public authoritative
Weather & satellite imagery, government regulations & label rules, commodity markets, peer-reviewed research.
Used forContext the client doesn't own. Compliance checks. Market signals.
Source 3 · Synthetic
Simulation data
LLM-generated scenarios built on top of real baselines. Always labeled as simulation output.
Used for"What-if" planning. Pre-connection demos. Never live recommendations.
!
The hard line. Synthetic data never grounds a real-world recommendation. If Telus asks whether our outputs are "made up by AI" — no, recommendations are grounded in Sources 1 & 2 only, and every output shows its sources.
