02 / 09 · the thesis
The thesis

Every regulator in Alberta already runs an inspection cycle.
Nobody runs an AI Q&A layer over the documents that cycle inspects.

Published corpus. Recurring inspection. Personally accountable operator. That pattern repeats across thousands of regulated workplaces from Calgary to Grande Prairie — stress-tested against five criteria.

Compliance buyers don't pay for "AI." They pay for "answer this question against my policy in 6 seconds before the inspector walks in."

Alberta is Canada's most regulation-dense province by operator count — ACP, WCB-Alberta, AER, seven health colleges, and 347 municipalities all running active inspection cycles. The addressable market is the entire province, and no incumbent AI tool covers any of it.

Calgary proves the density. AlgoPharma™ proves the model. Alberta proves the scale.

  1. AlgoPharma™ replicationArchitecture, doc schema, and Q&A patterns reused — not rebuilt from zero.
  2. Defensible moatRegulatory corpus that compounds value, not undercuttable by Lexis or generic LLMs.

  4. Alberta densityEnough regulated operators across the province to build a repeatable sales motion.
