01 · Purpose & Release Principles

Release Alignment

v1.0 is AlgoPharma™'s Minimum Sellable Product — not a prototype, but a polished, chargeable product that solves the core compliance workflow for Canadian pharmacy operators. Defining a fixed scope is how we actually ship.

Ship, Sell, Learn

The discipline of maintaining release scope ensures we can launch, generate revenue, and collect real-world feedback — instead of endlessly chasing "just one more feature."

MSP, Not MVP

v1.0 is polished enough to charge for. Every shipped feature must be complete, tested, and production-ready — no rough edges, no "we'll fix it post-launch."

Scope Stability

After 2026-03-30, the only permitted changes are bug fixes, accessibility improvements, copy corrections, and security patches. Any new feature request goes directly to the backlog.

Future Roadmap

v1.0 establishes the compliance foundation. Future releases will scale AlgoPharma™ into a comprehensive platform — always guided by customer feedback and strategic priorities.

Product Scope, Users, and Guardrails

To ensure clear alignment across all teams, the following defines exactly who our product serves and the central problems we are solving.

Target Audience

The primary users of AlgoPharma™ are Canadian pharmacy owners, Designated Managers (DMs), and compliance officers who are responsible for ensuring operations meet provincial college standards (e.g., OCP, ACP) and overriding federal guidelines (Health Canada, NAPRA).

Core Value Proposition (What The Product Solves)

AlgoPharma™ reduces the administrative burden of operating a compliant pharmacy. By centralizing regulatory intelligence, it accelerates the drafting of SOPs, simplifies incident reporting (CPhIR), and actively guides pharmacies through preparing for—or responding to—regulatory inspections.

Out of Scope

AlgoPharma™ is purpose-built as an operational compliance platform, not a clinical or patient-facing system. It excludes medical properties such as prescription handling, theranostics, and patient records, as well as pharmacy dispensing, EMR, point-of-sale, and advanced inventory tracking. It does not fulfill full CQI+ programs in this initial release. This deliberate scope ensures we stay focused on compliance workflows.

Human Oversight & AI Fallibility

The platform augments administrative workflows but does not replace the professional judgment of a licensed pharmacist. It cannot fully eliminate human error, and accountability for compliance adherence remains with the pharmacy’s designated manager. AI‑generated documents, chat responses, and regulatory interpretations may contain inaccuracies; all outputs must be critically reviewed and independently verified by a qualified compliance officer before adoption.

03 · Team Alignment

Responsibilities Post-Freeze

Every team works from this document as the single source of truth. No verbal scope changes are binding — all changes must be logged as a formal backlog item.

Engineering
  • Only bug fixes and approved polish changes after 2026-03-30
  • All new feature PRs must be tagged "post-v1.0" and not merged to main
  • Complete remaining QA items and load testing before target launch date
  • Freeze branch "release/v1.0" — no feature merges permitted
QA
  • Test all 34 in-scope features against acceptance criteria
  • Regression test after every bug-fix commit to release/v1.0
  • Document known limitations (not bugs) so Sales can position correctly
  • Sign-off required before public launch is authorised
Marketing
  • All landing page copy and feature lists must reflect v1.0 scope only
  • Do not advertise unshipped features
  • Prepare launch materials: demo video, case study (Strathcona Compounding), and email sequence
  • Coordinate launch date with Product for coordinated go-live
Sales
  • Be transparent about product limitations and deferred features when managing prospect expectations
  • All demos must use the demo data loader; do not promise custom integrations in v1.0
  • Feature requests from prospects → log in backlog; never promise timelines unilaterally
  • ISMP CPhIR direct submission is NOT in v1.0 — communicate clearly
