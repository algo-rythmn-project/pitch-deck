Why Now
Your 60-second regulatory argument

February 2025. Kebaowek.
The rules changed.

Kebaowek First Nation v. Canada · Federal Court FC 319 · February 2025
UNDRIP is now binding Canadian law for Crown consultation. Every resource project must maintain FPIC process records, community correspondence, impact-assessment responses and accommodation documentation — as a legal obligation, not a best practice.
Implication 01

A new document burden with no existing software to manage it. Law firms charge $500–$2,000/hour to handle FPIC records manually.

Implication 02

Every active mine and fertilizer plant is immediately affected — expansions, permit renewals, EA amendments all require post-Kebaowek compliance.

Implication 03

No possible incumbent. This obligation is a post-2025 regulatory creation. No platform existed before it; none has been built since.
