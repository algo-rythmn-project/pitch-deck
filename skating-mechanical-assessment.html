Skating Mechanical Assessment

Scoring Methodology & Biomechanical Reference

Sales & Marketing Resource Version 1.0

1. What Does This App Do?

This application analyses ice hockey skating mechanics from video footage. You upload a video of a player skating, and the app performs the following:

  • Detects all players in each frame.
  • Tracks them across frames so you can select the target player.
  • Estimates body pose (33 joint positions) using AI.
  • Measures biomechanical angles such as knee bend, trunk lean, and stance width.
  • Grades each posture criterion on an A–E scale (A = excellent, E = poor).
  • Maps grades to 12 on-ice ability scores (1–5 scale) like Agility, Speed, and Power.
  • Exports results as CSV, professional PDF reports, or annotated videos.

The output is a Score Card showing how well the player's skating mechanics support their on-ice performance.

2. How It Works (High-Level Pipeline)

1

Player Detection & Tracking

YOLOv8 detects people. ByteTrack assigns stable IDs so the system follows the right player throughout the clip.

2

Pose Estimation

MediaPipe detects 33 body landmarks in 3D (x, y, z coordinates) with visibility confidence scores.

3

Angle Computation

Biomechanical angles (e.g., Spine vs Vertical) are calculated using high-precision vector math.

4

Grading (A–E)

Measured angles are compared against professional coaching thresholds to assign intuitive letter grades.

5

Ability Score Mapping

Grades are mapped to 12 ability scores via an expert-defined scoring matrix.

6

Output & Visualization

A comprehensive Score Card with radar charts, per-frame analysis, and PDF export functionality.

3. The 5 Skating Phases

The system evaluates technique based on the specific skating maneuver being performed.

Skating Phase What It Looks Like When to Use
Forward Glide Player coasting on both skates, knees bent Baseline posture check
Forward Gliding Turn Player turning while gliding (wide arc) Evaluating turn mechanics
Tight Turns Player making sharp direction changes Evaluating tight-space agility
Forward Stride Player skating forward with full strides Evaluating stride power & form
Forward Crossovers Player crossing one foot over the other Evaluating crossover technique

3.1 Phase-Metric Comparison Example

Different skating maneuvers require focus on different body parts. For example, a Forward Stride focuses on explosive leg drive, while a Gliding Turn focuses on stance stability.

Phase: Forward Stride

  • Shoulders Ahead of Toes (Critical for drive)
  • Leg Extension (Measures push-off force)
  • Knee Inward (Check for stability faults)

Phase: Forward Gliding Turn

  • Foot Width (Stance stability)
  • Feet Past Midline (Turn commitment)
  • Outside Leg Angle (Push during rotation)

4. What Gets Measured

Key biomechanical metrics are extracted for every frame. Here is a selection of the core measurements:

Trunk Flexion Angle

Angle of spine (mid-shoulder to mid-hip) vs vertical. Indicates forward lean/drive.

Knee Angle

The bend angle at the knee joint. 180° = straight, <90°=deep engagement.

Leg Extension

Angle of the hip-to-ankle vector behind the body during push-off.

Stance Width Ratio

Ankle-to-ankle distance divided by hip width. Evaluates stability.

Shoulder Rotation

Difference in depth between shoulders. Measures stability and core engagement.

Knee Inward (Valgus)

Tracks if knees collapse inward, a common biomechanical fault.

8. Scoring Matrices

The core of the system is the Scoring Matrix. Every criterion (like Knee Bend) has different weights for each of the 12 abilities. For example, a deep knee bend contributes heavily to all 12 categories, whereas shoulder stability matters most for Puck Protection and Drive.

Note: We apply a multiplier based on the grade to the base score defined at Grade C (Average).

Grade Multiplier Effect
A × 1.67 Base scores increased ~67%
B × 1.33 Base scores increased ~33%
C × 1.00 Baseline (no change)
D × 0.67 Base scores reduced ~33%
E × 0.33 Base scores reduced ~67%

9. Worked Example: End-to-End Calculation

Scenario: Forward Stride

A player is evaluated for a Forward Stride. Their measurements come back as follows:

Measurement

Trunk Flexion: 35°

Result: Grade B (Multiplier 1.33)

Measurement

Leg Extension: 25°

Result: Grade D (Multiplier 0.67)

The system takes the baseline scores for "Agility" and "Power" and applies these multipliers. Even though the grades appear similar, Power remains higher (3.2 vs 2.6) because the Forward Stride phase puts more weight on Trunk Flexion and Leg Extension for Power than it does for Agility.

10. Multi-Frame Averaging

A athlete's score isn't just a snapshot. We analyze every single frame where a pose is detected and average the results for consistent technique assessment.

$$\text{Final Score}_{ability} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \text{score}_{ability,\text{frame}_i}$$

Where $N$ is the number of valid frames analyzed.
