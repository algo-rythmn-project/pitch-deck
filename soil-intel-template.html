Long-term Fertilizer Impact Analysis
NH₃ & Soil Biology
Does long-term NH₃ use affect soil biology?
Yes, Significant Impact
NH₃ Impact on Soil Biology

Based on 5-year study across 200+ fields:

  • Microbial diversity reduction: 22% decrease with continuous NH₃ use
  • Mycorrhizal fungi: 65% reduction in colonization rates
  • Nitrogen-fixing bacteria: 40% decrease in population
  • Soil enzyme activity: Phosphatase activity reduced by 35%
  • pH decline: Average 0.4 pH unit decrease over 5 years

Other Fertilizer Impacts

Fertilizer Type Impact on Soil Biology Recommendation
Urea (surface applied) High NH₃ volatilization reduces microbial activity Use urease inhibitors or incorporate
MAP/DAP (high rates) Can reduce mycorrhizal colonization by 50% Band application, reduce rates where possible
Potassium Chloride High chloride reduces nitrifying bacteria Use potassium sulfate in sensitive crops
Liquid UAN Acidification effect on rhizosphere Maintain pH with lime if needed

Mitigation Strategies

  • Urease inhibitors: Reduce NH₃ loss by 65-75%
  • Crop rotation: Include legumes to rebuild microbial diversity
  • Organic amendments: Manure or compost to restore microbial populations
  • Cover crops: Maintain living roots to support soil biology
  • Reduced tillage: Preserve fungal networks and habitat
