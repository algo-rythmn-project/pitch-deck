The regulatory shift

February 2025. Kebaowek.
The rules changed.

UNDRIP is now binding Canadian law for Crown consultation. Compliance document obligations that were best practice are now legal requirements — and no software platform was built to meet them.

Kebaowek First Nation v. Canada · Federal Court FC 319 · February 2025
Every resource project must maintain queryable FPIC process records, community correspondence, impact-assessment responses, and accommodation documentation — as a legal obligation, not a best practice.
A new document burdenLaw firms charge $500–$2,000/hr to handle FPIC records manually. No software manages it automatically.
Every active operation affectedPermit renewals, EA amendments, and expansions all require post-Kebaowek FPIC compliance immediately.
No incumbent existsThis obligation is a post-2025 regulatory creation. No platform was built for it before — or since.
