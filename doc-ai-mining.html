Why This Matters To You

You already live this problem.
Potash & fertilizer is the proof case.

Any organization facing significant regulatory issues — needing detailed, nuanced understanding, cross-referencing across hundreds of documents, and redundant report production — carries the same structural pain. Mining is simply the most extreme version of it.

01
The documents exist — but nobody can interrogate them. Environmental permits, tailings plans, FPIC consultation records, OHS procedures, closure plans. They sit in SharePoint as dead PDFs.
02
Cross-referencing is manual and fragile. Does this water-monitoring procedure satisfy the provincial permit, the federal requirement and the corporate ESG commitment? Today, a person reads three binders.
03
Report production is redundant and repeated. The same facts get re-written into regulator submissions, board reports, ESG disclosures and community updates — every quarter, by hand.
04
Audit and inspection create recurring fear. When the regulator says "show me," staff scramble through document stores hoping the evidence is current and complete.

The product category

One question separates a real opportunity from a crowded market —

Is there any platform today that lets users query their own compliance documents in plain language?

For mining, fertilizer, health, municipal, environmental and OHS, the answer is no. Today's software manages forms, dashboards and project files. None of it supplies the intelligence layer.

Whoever builds it first does not enter the category. They define it.

The Shared Architecture

One platform. One regulatory corpus.
Six industry products on top.

Each product looks bespoke to its industry, but they share an engine. The regulatory standards corpus is built once per jurisdiction; every client's own documents are queried against it. That is why the roadmap compounds — each product makes the next cheaper to ship.

01
FIRST LAYER

Ingest

The client's own compliance documents — permits, procedures, inspection records, consultation files — are parsed, structured and indexed. Messy PDFs become queryable knowledge.

02
SECOND LAYER

Cross-reference

A jurisdiction-specific regulatory corpus — federal acts, provincial regulations, association standards — is built once. Client documents are mapped and tested against it automatically.

03
THIRD LAYER

Answer & produce

Staff ask questions in plain language and get cited answers. Recurring reports — regulator submissions, board packs, ESG disclosures — are drafted from one source of truth.
