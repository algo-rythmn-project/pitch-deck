02 / 09 · the thesis
The thesis

Every regulator in Calgary already runs an inspection cycle.
Nobody runs an AI Q&A layer over the documents that cycle inspects.

Published corpus. Recurring inspection. Personally accountable operator. That pattern repeats in three Calgary sectors — stress-tested against five criteria.

Compliance buyers don't pay for "AI." They pay for "answer this question against my policy in 6 seconds before the inspector walks in."

That buyer exists in every regulated profession with a published corpus, a recurring inspection cycle, and an owner-operator who can sign a cheque. Calgary is the densest concentration of that buyer in Canada.

// The five tests every sector must pass
  1. AlgoPharma™ replicationArchitecture, doc schema, and Q&A patterns reused — not rebuilt from zero.
  2. Defensible moatRegulatory corpus that compounds value, not undercuttable by Lexis or generic LLMs.
  3. Short sales cycleOwner-operator buyer, not committee procurement. Decision in weeks, not quarters.
  4. Calgary densityEnough operators within a 60-minute drive to walk in cold, demo, and close.
  5. Hard ROI hookLicense risk, premium reduction, or audit penalty — quantifiable, not aspirational.
