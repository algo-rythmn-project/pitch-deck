● 02 / What each side brings

The fit isn't ambition — it's complementarity.

DigiFarm cleans the inputs. We model the outputs. Neither side has the other half today.

— ✦ —

DigiFarm → the input layer

Sentinel-2 super-resolved 10m → 1m. All spectral bands. 10-year archive, deeply resolved per growing season. Region-specific boundaries — 800M+ hectares ready, 7M+ hectares of training data. Phenology-stage segmentation. Global coverage. API-first, ~€0.10–0.20 / km² at scale.

Algo-Rythmn → the analytics layer

Root-cause modeling beyond correlation, drawn from drug-discovery methods. Multivariate, multi-dimensional time series with anomaly & outlier detection. Wavelet / frequency-domain transforms. Quant finance background — 200-factor models, PCA / ICA. Risk management precedent (AFSC). 10M acres analyzed last season.

What's missing on both sides

DigiFarm admits predictive yield only works in narrow genetics-owner cases today. We have the predictive stack but not their clean, regionally-calibrated input layer. Stitch the two and the result is defensible at scale.

● 03 / Three use cases for the pilot

Anchor, parallel, and the longer bet.

Each case is structured to play to both companies' strengths and to produce evidence inside one season — not three.

Case 01 / Anchor

Yield forecasting for the US co-op

The clearest path to a defensible, season-ready forecast.
Lead candidate North America Season-end signal

The pilot

Take the co-op's footprint. Ingest DigiFarm's deeply-resolved Sentinel-2 history plus region-specific boundaries as the clean input layer. Apply our time-series and root-cause models to predict end-of-season yield by field. Benchmark against the co-op's actual historical yield records.

Why both sides win

DigiFarm's own admission: predictive yield at scale is unsolved — only niche genetics owners pull it off. Bence specifically said one-meter pixels and accurate boundaries "clean up the data set quite a bit and increase the viability" of predictive modeling. That clean-up is exactly the input our root-cause stack needs.

What we ask DigiFarm for

Sample data on the co-op footprint: super-resolved historical scenes plus boundary set across the last 3–5 growing seasons. That's it.

What success looks like

A defensible per-field yield forecast 30–60 days before harvest, benchmarked field-by-field against ground truth, accurate enough to anchor a commercial product (see Case 03 and Product 02).

Case 02 / Parallel

In-season damage detection & insurance signal

Closer to live. Inside one season, not at year-end.
Insurance / reinsurance In-season AFSC precedent

The pilot

Run our multivariate anomaly detection — wavelet and frequency-domain — on the deep-resolution imagery stream at field-polygon level. Flag damage events as they happen: hail, flood, drought stress, disease. Output: an auditable damage signal usable for claims triage or parametric payouts.

Why both sides win

DigiFarm's biggest commercial pillar is insurance. They already do in-season risk analysis but it's bespoke pipeline-by-pipeline custom work today. Our anomaly stack productizes it. We bring AFSC risk-management precedent. Insurance cycles validate inside one season.

What we ask DigiFarm for

One representative region. Live deep-resolution feed for the active growing season plus the prior season as baseline. Boundary set with phenology stages.

What success looks like

Damage events detected at field level within days of occurrence, with false-positive rates low enough to be insurer-defensible. A demo deck for one of DigiFarm's existing insurance customers within the season.

Case 03 / Future bet

Regional yield → commodity hedging signal

The differentiated wedge no one else can credibly offer.
Commodities N. America + Brazil Phase 2

The pilot

Aggregate yield forecasts from Case 01 across a representative sample of homogeneous regions in North America and Brazil — per Bence's own suggestion to "take samples from different homogeneous regions." Combine with our existing commodity quant models to produce a regional production forecast tradeable as a hedging signal.

Why both sides win

DigiFarm's global coverage becomes a moat — no US-only ag-tech can do this. Our finance DNA becomes a moat — no satellite company can do this. Kenny's framing on the call: "transparency of commodity trading is welcome." That's the commercial wedge.

Posture for now

Mention as the strategic direction in our reply — don't ask DigiFarm to scope this yet. It justifies the partnership being broader than a one-off project. Scope formally only after Case 01 has proof points.

What success looks like

By season's end: a regional yield forecast feeding a paper-traded hedging signal we can show to one commodity desk for validation. The narrative for a Phase 2 raise or co-developed product.
