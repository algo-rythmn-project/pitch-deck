§ 03 — Deep Dives

The plays in detail.

01 Trading · Lead bet · Exporters & Millers

Hedging AI Assistant

A closed-loop replacement for the Excel-plus-trader-intuition workflow that dominates Brazilian agribusiness treasury today.

The Pitch

An agentic layer that ingests CBOT and B3 prices, FX feeds, weather data, freight rates, and the company's physical position, then executes hedge actions against pre-approved policy guardrails. Not a dashboard — closed-loop execution.

Why It Pilots Fast

  • Data inputs are licensed market feeds. No proprietary integration.
  • Single CFO or Head of Trading sponsors. No committee.
  • Success metric is unambiguous: basis points captured.
  • Rabobank, Uhedge, PwC have spent eighteen months publishing exactly this thesis. Buyers are pre-educated.

First-Call Targets

São Martinho · Raízen · Amaggi (trading desk) · Inpasa · JBS (feed procurement) · São Paulo arms of Bunge, Cargill, ADM

90-Day Scope

Single commodity (soy or sugar), single book. Read-only execution recommendations weeks 1–6, supervised execution weeks 7–12. Threshold: outperform existing book by 25bps.

On a USD 500M annual soy book, a 50bp improvement in hedge timing returns USD 2.5M / year. Pilot at USD 100K fixed; expand to 10–20bps performance share on subsequent books.

02 Trading · Lead bet · Grain Traders

Basis & FOB Pricing Engine

One of the most undertooled high-value workflows in Brazilian agribusiness today — the spread between Chicago futures and physical delivery at Brazilian ports.

The Pitch

The basis between CBOT futures and physical FOB delivery at Paranaguá, Santos, or Itaqui moves constantly with logistics, weather, FX, and Chinese demand. Traders price this with spreadsheets and gut instinct. We build the agent that prices basis in real-time and executes hedges across the spread.

Why It Pilots Fast

  • Single trader or desk head can sponsor. No IT integration in v1.
  • Output is a live basis price; comparison against manual pricing is immediate.
  • Strategically aligned with Felipe's Canadian-Chinese network — most basis flow is to China.

First-Call Targets

Amaggi · Cargill Brasil · Bunge Brasil · COFCO Brasil · Viterra · Olam · ADM Brasil · Caramuru · Coamo trading

90-Day Scope

Single port (Paranaguá), single commodity (soy). Parallel-run against trader's manual pricing for sixty days; supervised execution in the last thirty. Threshold: equal or better basis capture with 50% less time.

A trader handling 2 Mt / year of soy with a 5¢/bushel basis-capture improvement returns USD 3.7M / year. A pilot fee of USD 150K is trivially recoverable.

03 Trading · Prop Desks & Hedge Funds

Cross-Exchange Arbitrage Agent

Brazil sits at the structural center of a triangle between US supply and Chinese demand — and the Canadian-Chinese network framing makes Algo-Rythmn unusually credible to buyers in this space.

The Pitch

Soybean and corn futures trade on CME (Chicago), B3 (São Paulo), and Dalian (China). Latency, FX, and basis arbitrage opportunities exist across the three but require fast multi-venue execution. The agentic layer routes, sequences, and executes.

Why It Pilots Fast

  • Prop desks pay for performance — structure as performance share, zero upfront.
  • Time-to-value measured in days once data feeds connect.
  • Smaller buyer set (10–20 firms) but each ticket is large.

First-Call Targets

XP commodity desk · BTG Pactual agribusiness fund · specialized commodity hedge funds (Pacifico, Kapitalo agri sleeve) · trading desks at Cargill, Bunge, COFCO, Amaggi

90-Day Scope

Two commodities (soy + corn), three exchanges. Paper-trading mode for forty-five days; live execution with capped notional in last forty-five. Threshold: positive Sharpe ratio above 1.0.

A prop desk running USD 50M of agricultural exposure across three exchanges with 100bps annualized arbitrage capture returns USD 500K / year. Performance share at 25% returns USD 125K from a single small desk.

04 AgTech · Cooperatives

Cooperative Member Intelligence

Brazilian cooperatives finance, supply, and trade for thousands of farmer-members — and today they do this on Excel and tribal knowledge.

The Pitch

Cooperatives need year-ahead member-level forecasts: yield, default risk, input demand, and trading position. We provide the agentic forecasting and decision layer, which the co-op uses internally and can resell to members as a premium service.

Why It Pilots Fast

  • Innovation budgets are pre-allocated annually; spending happens fast once approved.
  • Single Diretor de Inovação or Diretor Comercial can sponsor.
  • Co-ops compete intensely on member services — real urgency to differentiate.

First-Call Targets

Coamo (largest co-op, currently expanding) · C.Vale · Cocamar · Frísia · Lar · Sicredi (financial co-op with major agribusiness arm)

90-Day Scope

Single co-op, member subset of 200–500. Focus on default risk plus input demand forecasting in v1; skip yield and trading. Threshold: forecast accuracy beats existing Excel model by 15% on holdout.

A co-op with 5,000 members and USD 1B trading volume can capture USD 3–8M / year through better aggregate position sizing, default prediction, and input procurement. Pilot at USD 75K; scale to USD 50/member/year SaaS.

05 Finance · Banks & Asset Managers

CRA / Fiagro Risk Engine

Brazil has built a fifty-billion-dollar market in agribusiness receivables — and no one models counterparty, collateral, and weather risk in an integrated way today.

The Pitch

CRAs (Certificados de Recebíveis do Agronegócio) and Fiagros (agribusiness investment funds) each carry non-trivial counterparty, collateral, and weather risk. We build the agentic risk engine: continuous repricing, default-probability scoring, climate-driven collateral revaluation.

Why It Pilots Fast

  • CVM has pushed for better disclosure — regulatory tailwind.
  • Single risk officer or portfolio manager sponsors. No committee in v1.
  • Higher technical bar than Plays 1–3, but smaller competitive set.

First-Call Targets

Itaú BBA agribusiness · Rabobank Brasil · Banco do Brasil rural credit · Bradesco agribusiness · asset managers running Fiagros (Vinci, XP Asset, Itaú Asset agri sleeve)

90-Day Scope

Single Fiagro fund or CRA portfolio. Retrospective backtest in weeks 1–6 against actual default and price events of last three years; live monitoring in weeks 7–12. Threshold: identify at least two risk events the existing model missed.

An asset manager running USD 500M in Fiagro exposure with a 50bp reduction in unexpected losses returns USD 2.5M / year. Pilot at USD 150K fixed; expand to ongoing data subscription.

§ 04 — Sequencing

Build credibility first, then expand.

Plays #3 and #5 have the largest TAM and the highest margins. Neither pilots in ninety days from a cold start — prop desks need a track record; banks need procurement and risk-committee approval. So we build credibility with #1, #2, and #4 first, then expand.

Months 1–3
Pre-conference
#1 + #2
Both are commodity-trading plays sharing the same data infrastructure (CBOT, B3, FX, weather, freight). Building one builds 70% of the other. Sell to overlapping buyers.
Months 4–6
Post-conference
Add #4
Cooperative play uses different data (member-level + agronomic) but the same agentic engine. Diversifies revenue base away from trading-cycle volatility.
Months 7–12
Add #3 and #5
Once the platform has 3–4 reference customers, harder financial-sector buyers (prop desks, banks) become accessible. Use early customers as references.
