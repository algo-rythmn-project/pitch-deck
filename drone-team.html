01 / Why

By the time you see it from the truck, it's already cost you money.

Most crop trouble — disease pockets, nutrient gaps, water stress — starts in small patches you can't see from the road. By the time it's visible across the field, the patch has spread, the window to react has closed, and a chunk of your yield is already gone for good.

The lag

A week of waiting costs real money.

Disease and stress don't pause between scouting trips. Every week you wait to spot a problem, more of the loss becomes permanent — you can't get it back, even if you treat the patch later.

The blind spots

A truck and a pair of boots can't cover 200+ acres.

Walking the field is slow. Driving the field misses the inside. By the time you spot the trouble from the headlands, the problem patch is usually 2–3× larger than when it started.

The opportunity

Most of the loss is still recoverable — if you catch it in time.

Across our test field, roughly 70% of the patch loss was still recoverable in week one. By week four, that share had dropped sharply. Early information is the difference between a fix and a write-off.

03 / Method

Why our drones find more, in less time.

Standard drone scouting

A drone that mows back-and-forth, treating every square foot the same.

  • Burns flight time on the boring, healthy parts of the field
  • Misses early-stage patches that are only a few square metres
  • Long flights, more batteries, more pilot time, more cost
  • You get a pretty picture, not an answer
Our drone team

Drones that spend their battery where the trouble actually is.

  • Same patches found in ~40% less flight time
  • Or in the same flight time, more patches found
  • Fewer batteries, less labour, lower cost per acre
  • You get a short report telling you what to do

It's not about more drones. It's about smarter ones.

05 / Transparency

Where the dollar figures come from.

No black box. The loss numbers in your report are built on the same approach your agronomist already knows — and calibrated to your specific field.

The short version

Patch size × severity × your crop price = potential loss. That's the industry-standard damage-function approach used by university extension services and the Crop Protection Network. We then split that loss into "still recoverable" and "already permanent" using a recovery model that we calibrate against your own field over the first 1–2 flights.

09 / FAQ

A few of the questions you might have.

Do I need to learn any software?
No. We fly the drones, we process the images, we send you the report. You get a PDF, a map link, and a phone number to call if anything needs explaining.
How long does a weekly flight take?
About 40 minutes of flight time for a field around the size of our test field (234 acres). The drones land themselves, and nothing on the ground has to stop or move.
What kind of "problems" can it actually spot?
Disease pockets, water stress, nutrient deficiency, and unusual growth patterns. Every patch comes with a best-guess at the cause and a severity score, so you and your agronomist can decide what to do.
How are the "potential loss" and "recoverable" numbers calculated?
The core — patch size × severity × your crop price — is the standard damage-function approach used by university extension services and the Crop Protection Network. Your agronomist will recognize it. The recoverable / permanent split uses our own recovery model, which we calibrate to your own field over the first 1–2 flights. We're happy to share the model details with your agronomist on request.
What if my crop price or yield baseline changes mid-season?
We refresh those inputs every flight. The recovery curves are also recalibrated against ground-truth from your own field after the first 1–2 visits, so the dollar figures get more accurate to your operation, not less, as the season progresses.
What's a realistic starting point?
A one-field, four-week pilot during your most pressure-sensitive part of the season. You get four weekly reports, a season-end summary, and a side-by-side comparison with your usual scouting. No multi-year commitment.
